The main thing I want to focus on is design. One of the things I would complain to Sharky about when it came to Rend's repo was a lack of design documentation. There is actually a fair bit for some of the functions and how things hook together, but what I mean is game design. I think that's a pretty severe problem with a codebase that runs on crowdsourced volunteer contributions, it'll inevitably lead to some drift from what the original aim was I'll use the Monsters as an example. Does anyone know what the Monster faction is supposed to do? Last I checked (this may have been patched) they can joint with the Mafia and hostile thirds, so why aren't these roles just grouped under Mafia? Something to keep in mind is that before BM launched, Rend's Arcmafia/epicmafia.org didn't group the Cultists and Cthulhu under the Monsters. It was just Lycan and Witch. All we have for what Rend intended the Monsters to be is the following: "Monsters meet together and win if they reach the majority just like the mafia, but they do not vote to kill someone each night." Okay, that's simple, but since then the Monsters have gained the ability to directly kill people: the Thing and Alchemist both have direct ability to kill, and the Leech is a bit of a slow burn but it still kills directly. I'm not saying I'm against the Monsters having killing power necessarily, but most versions of Mafia/Werewolf that have a second scum faction are explicitly hostile-but-not-killing, which comes as both a challenge and a benefit. Why is the Thing a Monster when on EM, werewolf made for a fine independent role that was just a variant on Serial Killer?
golbolco 05/18/2023 10:54 AM
Another problem: bloat I think that we're starting to see the accretion of roles that have such minor differences they only exist to take up space. Messenger versus Fabulist is a great example: the latter is intended to fake system messages, while the former has a preset "You received a message" text preceding whatever they send a player. Is this really necessary? Seems like Messenger is inherently more limiting than Fabulist. You can use Fabulist to either fake system messages or just send whatever you want, nobody can stop you (btw this whole thing goes for Courier and Hoaxer too, obviously) Medic is a Doctor that can self-visit. This is archaic design in the face of BM's best innovation for game design: modifiers. Let's say that no role can self-visit by default (just hypothetically I know that will impact certain roles). Give that role a "Selfish" modifier and all of a sudden you don't need roles like Medic. The problem with roles like Medic is that they inevitably start to invite variants on other roles in the same mold. During the last days of EM, Lucid decided that since Nurse is a Doctor that can roleblock, he wanted to add a Cop that could roleblock. He called it Friendly Cop. Imagine if this just became standard: we would have a Friendly Tracker, a Friendly Watcher, a Friendly Detective all cluttering up the role page which has already started to become innavigable. Wouldn't a roleblocking modifier cover all of our bases? And btw: just because we remove roles like Medic or Friendly Cop or Nurse in favor of modifiers doesn't mean they stop existing. You just have to make a Doctor or a Cop or a whatever with the modifier of your choice. Same goes for Bulletproof: why does Bulletproof exist as a default role in the era where we can have a Villager (armored)?
10:57

This is already done over on MafiaUniverse and Town of Salem, should not be hard to do. Put crafting roles in one category, investigatory roles in another category, miscellaneous roles in another category etc etc etc This is just good for new players and helps them learn stuff, and it also keeps things from getting a little haphazard and random with the organization on the page in the first place.
